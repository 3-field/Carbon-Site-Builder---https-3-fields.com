AND

Abandon Normal Devices (AND) is a commissioning agency and a catalyst for new approaches to art-making and digital invention. We create ground-breaking projects which challenge the definitions of art and moving image with a distinct emphasis on creative enquiry and provocations. Since its inception in 2009, AND has shaken the creative sector through radical, risk-taking projects that challenge conventional modes of participation, and stimulate new art forms and creative inquiry. AND brings together an eclectic mix of academics, filmmakers, scientists and anarchists to actively push the boundaries of audience experience and arts production. Inviting artists to hijack the imagination, by developing projects which abandon traditional settings and partnerships. Proposing a significant shift in the way art is experienced, the AND portfolio consists of site-specific film happenings, exhibitions, performances, online projects, residencies, public realm interventions and a roaming biennial festival. Audiences are invited to interact with bold ideas, encounter new art-forms and experience art, in the everyday and in unexpected locations.

Quicksand/Unbox

Fak'ugesi Fak'ugesi African Digital Innovation Festival is located at the doorstep of the University of Witwatersrand, is the flagship event at the Tshimologong Digital Innovation Hub, celebrating diverse disciplines within the Digital Creative Industries (DCIs) such as gaming, animation, music, and XR.
The festival shines a spotlight not only on South Africa but on the entire African continent. Now in its 11th year, the theme #UNTILUNLOCKED reflects its ongoing mission to empower talent through learnerships, incubations, and residencies. Fak'ugesi also focuses on building critical infrastructure by actively engaging stakeholders and policymakers while awarding and celebrating digital innovation that elevates Johannesburg, Gauteng, and Africa on a global stage. This mission resonates with its passionate motto: Until, Until, UntilUnlocked!
Fak'ugesi Interests: Empowering talent, fostering infrastructure development, stakeholder engagement, celebrating digital innovation, and amplifying Africa's digital creative industries.

SOUTH AFRICA: Samukelisiwe Siphesihle Dube

is a Black, queer multidisciplinary artist and practising tattoo artist from the province of Mpumalanga. Deeply influenced by her late grandmother, she and her mother recreated her garden as a tribute and space for healing. Her work explores nature, grief, and identity through printmaking and plant growth on handmade paper.
